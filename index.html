FTC Turreted Shooter System (V1–V3)

This shooter system went through three major iterations, each addressing mechanical inconsistencies, friction losses, compression tuning, and turret integration. The evolution focused on improving accuracy, recovery time, and shot repeatability.

Shooter V1 — Double Flywheel

V1 was a vertical double-flywheel shooter using dual 6000 RPM motors. It achieved high rotational speed and low backspin, but suffered from:

  • Inconsistent compression → inconsistent trajectories
  • No angle adjustment → limited scoring positions
  • High friction in wheel alignment
  • Heavy frame → not turret-friendly
Shooter V2 — Adjustable Hood + Counter Roller

V2 introduced an adjustable hood and counter-roller system. Improvements included:

  • Adjustable hood → dynamic shot angles
  • Counter-roller → reduced backspin + smoother feeding
  • Better compression tuning

Issues discovered:

  • Hood pivot friction → slow rebound
  • Compression drift at high RPM
  • Belt tension inconsistencies
