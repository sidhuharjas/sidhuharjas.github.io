Mechanisms Overview

I compete in the FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC), where every season introduces a brand‑new game with new scoring rules and engineering challenges. This page gives a simple story of the mechanisms I built across the 2024–2025 and 2025–2026 seasons and why they existed.

2025–2026 — DECODE™

DECODE revolves around collecting 5‑inch spherical artifacts and shooting them into corner goals. Accuracy and consistency matter a lot, especially from different angles on the field.

Turreted Shooter System

My main engineering focus this season was developing a turreted shooter — a system that rotates independently of the drivetrain and fires artifacts accurately from anywhere on the field.

Tipper Park Mechanism

To earn the Base ranking point, robots must fit into a small zone at the end of the match. Full lift systems were too slow and unreliable, so I built a compact tipping mechanism that folds the robot’s footprint in half. I ran finite element analysis (FEA) to confirm the kicker geometry was strong while keeping weight low.

2024–2025 — INTO THE DEEP™

INTO THE DEEP required robots to pick up triangular samples, lift them to different heights, and place them into baskets or onto bars. My work focused on everything that happened after the intake.

Linear Extension System

A servo‑powered horizontal extension riding on a linear slide, geared 1:2.6 using two Axon Max servos. Built to reach scoring heights on the Submersible and Chamber.

Transfer System

This mechanism went through several versions — passive prototypes, LEGO testing, hybrid designs — before becoming a lightweight micro‑servo claw that reliably handed off samples for scoring.

Claw Geometry

The claw was shaped to match the inside of the FTC sample. Herringbone gears helped the fingers self‑center and grip smoothly without slipping.

2‑Bar / Virtual 4‑Bar

A simple linkage powered by an Axon Mini through a 1:1 gear. It moved samples from the intake to scoring positions, with an extra pitch joint to fine‑tune the drop‑off angle.

2025–2026 Season Overview — DECODE™

This season centered around fast, accurate shooting and consistent endgame performance. My work focused on developing the turreted shooter system and the tipping park mechanism.

2024–2025 Season Overview — INTO THE DEEP™

This season required controlled placement of samples into baskets and onto bars. I built the mechanisms that handled the entire scoring path after the intake.

Turreted Shooter System

A rotating shooter designed for accurate scoring from multiple field positions. Work included compression tuning, hood geometry, roller alignment, friction reduction, and turret integration.

Tipper Park Mechanism

A compact tipping mechanism that folds the robot’s footprint in half to secure the Base ranking point. FEA was used to validate strength while minimizing weight. Two bearing‑roller supports reduce friction during deployment.
